Faults in PM6 (Home)

Faults that are unlikely to be corrected by more parameterization

  • The heat of formation contribution from yne groups is too negative by ~10 kcal/mol. Thus acetylene is in error by -9.6 kcal/mol and diacetylene by -26.2 kcal/mol.
  • The twist angle of substituted biphenyls is too large.
  • The lowest energy form of acetophenone is twisted by ~12 degrees.  The barrier to going through the planar form is ~ 0.01 kcal/mol.
  • The DHf of cubane is too negative by 42.2 kcal/mol (this is the largest error of the well-behaved compounds)
  • Many elements that normally have two ligands can have more.  Thus H2S is predicted to form a stable dimer.

 

Faults that are likely to be corrected by more parameterization

  • Many core-core interactions are missing.  This is due to a lack of data and lack of interest.  Thus the core-core term for Cs-Sr is missing, because no examples of compounds containing a Cs-Sr bond have been found. Individual cases can be corrected easily, once suitable data are available.
  • The weak non-polar interaction (Van der Waals interaction) is under represented.  This shows up in p-stacking complexes, where the VdW interaction is about 70% too small.
  • The hydrogen bond distance in ice is about 0.1 Angstroms too small.  This results in ice having a predicted density of 1.07 instead of the observed 0.92.
  • Transition metal carbonyls frequently have the wrong symmetry, thus C4v Fe(CO)5 is predicted to be more stable than D3h.
