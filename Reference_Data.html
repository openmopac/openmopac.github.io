Sources for Reference Data (Home)

 

Heats of Formation   

The NIST WebBook is a good source. When requesting a heat of formation, remember to specify gas phase and kcal/mol.  The default is kJ/mol.
Mineral structures

 The American Mineralogist Crystal Structure Database is very good.  Watch for non-stoichimetric minerals, and positional and structural disorder. 

Atomic Energy Levels The Atomic Spectra Database has atomic energy levels for most elements and their unipositive ions.  Note that physicist's, not chemist's, notation is used, so the neutral system is M(I) and the unipositive ion is M(II).  Before these levels can be used, relativistic effects (spin-orbit coupling) must be removed.
Protein crystal structures Look at the PDB, the Protein Data Bank.  The PDB is also available on CDs, but the on-line collection is easier to use in practice.
Small organic compounds The best source is the Cambridge Structural Database.  This link is to information on how to acquire the CSD collection.
